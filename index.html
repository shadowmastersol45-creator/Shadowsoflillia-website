The Game

A Kingdom Lost.
A Princess in Hiding.
Eight Years Later.

Shadows of Lillia is a third-person action RPG built around one feeling — amazement, happiness, and uneasiness, all at once. A world so beautiful it stops you mid-movement just to look. And something underneath, felt before it is seen.

One sword. One story. Seven regions. The most unforgettable game we can make.

The World

A World That Existed Before You Arrived

Seven distinct regions, each with its own identity, its own danger, its own beauty. From the frozen north where Colissa has been hiding, to the fallen kingdom that waits at the end of everything.

No yellow paint. No waypoints. Light, pathways, and composition guide you naturally — every choice feels like your own.

The Frozen North

Where Colissa Has Been Hiding

Cold, beautiful, and full of things that glow in the dark. The starting region. The place she has called home for eight years while the world changed around her.

Something specific happens here. Today is the day she stops hiding.

"Amazement. Happiness. Uneasiness. All three, simultaneously."

The Design Philosophy

Exploration

Every Corner Worth Finding

Explore. Find things. Fight. A core loop built on the belief that encounters should be meaningful — not constant. Every enemy placed with intent. Every path worth walking.

Inspired by the worlds of Hollow Knight and Breath of the Wild — places that feel alive long after you've left them.

The Story

The Fall of Lillia

The Shadow Master walks toward the castle gates. Nothing stops him. The General fights and loses. The King chooses to stand rather than flee. The Captain takes young Colissa away on the King's orders.

Eight years later, Colissa is 18. She has watched the world change. Something happens. Today is the day.

The Golden Desert

Seven Regions.
One Destination.

Vast open desert. Frozen northern forests. Cursed mountains where it is always night. Ancient forests. A Japan-inspired region untouched by the Shadow Master. And at the end — Lillia Castle. The fallen kingdom.

Maximum scope: Ocarina of Time. AAA quality where it matters, ruthlessly selective about everything else.

Combat

One Sword.
Mastered Completely.

Tight, responsive combat inspired by Hollow Knight. Skill expression through timing — not button combinations. The dodge is also the parry. The same input at different levels of mastery produces different results.

Fast, grounded, anime-style movement. Trained on the run. Unpredictable.
